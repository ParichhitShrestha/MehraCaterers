About

Subhash discovered his love for cooking at the age of 19, while working alongside his skilled Mama-ji, a true master in the culinary world. He was drawn to the simplicity of food, the delicate balance of local ingredients, and the skillful use of spices.

His creativity and innovation is deeply rooted in Basholi Art, the world-renowned miniature art form from his hometown where he was born and raised.

Starting his culinary journey at Le Meridian New Delhi in 1990, Chef Mehra trained under industry giants such as Chef Manjit Singh Gill and Chef Davinder Kumar. Over four decades, Subhash refined his skills and ventured into diverse lands, including Italy, France, Spain, Malaysia, and more.

Guided by a mantra of "discovery and constant innovation," Chef Mehra ventured to the UK in May 1997. Climbing the ranks, he eventually opened his first restaurant, The Spice of London, in the coveted Covent Garden, earning a devoted following from guests.

Despite his extensive culinary expertise across various cuisines, Chef Mehra has a profound affection for the flavors of his hometown, Basholi in Jammu and Kashmir, India. Celebrated as the ambassador of Pahadi cuisine, he offers a unique blend of flavors that capture the essence of the mountains.

Born in 1964 in the lap of the majestic Himalayas and in the banks of Ravi River, Subhash carries the blessings of Baba AmarNath and Mata BaisnoDevi. His culinary journey transcends mere food; it's about creating an immersive experience that transports people to the heart of the mountains. Chef Subhash Mehra warmly expresses, "I take people behind the scenes to give them a real taste of the mountains. It's all about creating an experience."
